JEE Mains: Everything You Need to Know

If you're planning to pursue a career in engineering, the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) Mains is a must-take exam. Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA), JEE Mains is a computer-based exam that assesses a candidate's knowledge in physics, chemistry, and mathematics.

The JEE Mains exam is conducted twice a year, in January and April, and candidates can choose to appear for either or both exams. The exam is divided into two papers – Paper 1 for engineering aspirants and Paper 2 for architecture and planning aspirants.

The JEE Mains exam is a highly competitive exam, with lakhs of candidates appearing for it every year. To crack the exam, candidates need to have a strong foundation in the subjects covered and should be well-versed in problem-solving and time management skills.

JEE Mains Syllabus

The JEE Mains syllabus is based on the NCERT curriculum for classes 11 and 12. The topics covered in the exam include:

  • Physics - Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Electromagnetism, Optics, and Modern PhysicsMore Details
  • Chemistry - Physical Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, and Inorganic ChemistryMore Details
  • Mathematics - Algebra, Trigonometry, Coordinate Geometry, Calculus, and VectorsMore Details

JEE Mains Exam Pattern

The JEE Mains exam consists of 90 questions, with each question carrying four marks. For every incorrect answer, one mark is deducted from the total score. The exam is conducted in online mode and has a duration of three hours. The paper 1 exam has three sections – Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, while the paper 2 exam has two sections – Mathematics and Aptitude Test.

JEE Mains Preparation Tips

Preparing for the JEE Mains exam requires dedication and hard work. Here are some tips that can help you crack the exam:

  • Have a clear understanding of the syllabus and exam pattern
  • Make a study plan and stick to it
  • Practice as many sample papers and mock tests as possible
  • Revise regularly and focus on your weak areas
  • Stay calm and focused during the exam
